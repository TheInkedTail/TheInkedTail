About Michael Garcia

The mind behind Tail of the Stray

Michael Garcia writes character-driven fantasy for readers who want to live in a world, not merely visit it. Every story begins the same way: long before a single scene is written, he is mapping the lore, the economics, the politics, and the rules of magic — laying the foundations of a place solid enough to walk through.

His debut novel, Tail of the Stray, grew out of an unlikely seed: the backstory of a Dungeons & Dragons character named Velrik. A young vulpin rogue with a past he can't outrun, Velrik was meant to fill a single campaign sheet. Instead he opened a door onto the fractured nations and ancient magic of Eldrin — and Michael has been charting that land ever since.

"A good story isn't an escape from reality. It's an invitation into another one — explored out of pure curiosity."

That love of immersion was forged at the gaming table and in story-rich worlds like The Witcher and Baldur's Gate. From them he learned that the most memorable settings are the ones that feel lived-in — where history presses on the present and even small corners hint at something larger. He builds his worlds the same way: outline first, then deepen and complicate them as the characters move through.

When he isn't writing or buried in worldbuilding notes, you'll find him gaming, coding, out on a trail, or following wherever the music leads — metal, anime soundtracks, and the occasional burst of polka, sometimes all in the same afternoon.
